# Professor Email Reply

Handles replies to lecturers, supervisors, and professors with academic-appropriate tone and structure.

## Use when

- The recipient is academic staff.
- The user needs to reply with a request, clarification, update, apology, thanks, or scheduling message.
- The message should sound respectful and institutionally appropriate.

## Not for

- Casual teammate or group-project replies.
- Pure thread summarization.
- Generic reply polishing when no academic-specific considerations matter.

## Workflow

1. Identify the academic relationship and likely expectations.
2. Determine whether the reply is asking, clarifying, updating, thanking, or apologizing.
3. Draft or refine the reply with a clear structure:
   - acknowledgment
   - main response
   - any request or next step
   - polite close
4. Keep the tone respectful, concise, and easy to respond to.
5. Preserve names, dates, and institutional details accurately.

## Writing rules

- Avoid overly casual phrasing.
- Do not over-explain unless context clearly requires it.
- Keep requests specific and easy to answer.
- Return only the final reply unless the user asks for variants.

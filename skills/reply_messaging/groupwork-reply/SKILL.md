# Groupwork Reply

Handles replies in group-project or teammate coordination contexts.

## Use when

- The user is replying to teammates, collaborators, or project peers.
- The topic involves deadlines, task ownership, progress, blockers, scheduling, or coordination.
- The main goal is to move shared work forward clearly.

## Not for

- Formal replies to lecturers, supervisors, or professors.
- Summarizing the group discussion without replying.
- Extracting tasks only.
- Polishing an existing reply when the main issue is wording rather than coordination.

## Workflow

1. Identify the coordination issue: ownership, deadline, progress, blocker, or decision.
2. Draft a reply that makes responsibilities and next steps explicit.
3. Keep the tone collaborative and direct.
4. Reduce ambiguity around who will do what and by when.
5. If the context is unclear, ask a focused clarifying question rather than pretending certainty.

## Writing rules

- Be explicit about actions, timing, and ownership.
- Prefer collaborative wording over overly formal language.
- Avoid vague commitments.
- Return only the final reply unless the user asks for alternatives.
